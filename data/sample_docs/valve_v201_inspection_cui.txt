EQUIPMENT_TAG: V-201
DATE: 2024-06-15

--- 1. INSPECTION DETAILS ---
Equipment: V-201 (Unit-3 Control Valve)
Service: Hydrocarbon Gas
Insulation Type: Mineral Wool with Aluminum Jacketing
Reason for Inspection: Follow-up on operator observation of external rust on jacketing (O-LOG-2024-06). Suspected Corrosion Under Insulation (CUI).
Inspector: R. Davies (Level II NDT)
Method: Pulsed Eddy Current (PEC) and Visual (after partial stripping)

--- 2. FINDINGS ---
2.1 Visual Inspection
The aluminum jacketing on the lower body of valve V-201 showed water staining and minor pitting. The caulking around the valve stem protrusion was degraded, allowing water ingress during washdowns. 
Upon stripping a 150mm section of insulation, severe localized corrosion and scale build-up were observed on the carbon steel valve body.

2.2 PEC Wall Thickness Measurements
Nominal Wall Thickness: 12.5 mm
Minimum Acceptable Thickness (Tmin): 8.5 mm

Measurements at lower body (suspect area):
- Point 1: 9.2 mm
- Point 2: 7.8 mm (FAIL - below Tmin)
- Point 3: 8.1 mm (FAIL - below Tmin)
- Point 4: 9.5 mm

Average metal loss in the affected area is approximately 35%. 

--- 3. COMPLIANCE ASSESSMENT ---
This finding represents a significant compliance gap with API 570 (Piping Inspection Code) and OISD-116.
Condition: AMBER/RED - Immediate action required. The localized thinning compromises the pressure containment envelope of V-201.

--- 4. RECOMMENDATIONS ---
1. Immediate: De-rate the line pressure for V-201 by 20% to increase safety margin.
2. Short Term (within 7 days): Schedule a replacement of the V-201 valve body during the upcoming mini-turnaround. 
3. Long Term: Revise the insulation design for V-201 to use cellular glass instead of mineral wool, and apply a high-temperature epoxy coating (e.g., TSA - Thermal Sprayed Aluminum) to prevent future CUI.
4. Fleet-wide: Inspect all other insulated control valves in Unit-3 using the same NDT method within the next 30 days.
