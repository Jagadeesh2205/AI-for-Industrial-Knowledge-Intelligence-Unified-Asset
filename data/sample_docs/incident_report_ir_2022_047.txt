INCIDENT INVESTIGATION REPORT
============================
Report Number: IR-2022-047
Date of Incident: 14/09/2022
Date of Report: 28/09/2022
Classification: Near Miss / Equipment Failure

================================================================
1. INCIDENT SUMMARY
================================================================

Equipment: Centrifugal Pump P-101 (Grundfos CR 95-3)
Location: Unit-3, Ground Floor, Bay-7
Time of Incident: 02:45 AM (Night Shift)

Description:
Pump P-101 experienced a catastrophic bearing failure during normal operation.
The failure resulted in shaft seizure, mechanical seal damage, and an
uncontrolled leak of process fluid (hot water at 85°C) from the seal area.

Consequences:
- Equipment damage: Both bearings destroyed, mechanical seal damaged, shaft scored
- Process impact: Unit-3 process upset for 6 hours (switched to standby P-102)
- Safety: No personnel injuries (area was unoccupied at time of failure)
- Environmental: ~50 liters of hot water leaked, contained by bund area
- Financial: Estimated repair cost ₹4.5 lakhs + production loss ₹12 lakhs

3. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS (5-WHY METHOD)
================================================================

WHY 1: Why did the pump fail?
→ Catastrophic bearing failure (both DE and NDE bearings)

WHY 2: Why did the bearings fail?
→ Bearings were operated beyond their rated life.
  Last replacement: March 2022 (6 months prior).
  BUT: Bearings had been installed refurbished (not new OEM).
  Refurbished bearing life: ~6 months (vs 12 months for new).

WHY 3: Why were refurbished bearings used?
→ OEM bearings were out of stock at time of last PM.
  Procurement approved local refurbished alternative to avoid extended downtime.

WHY 4: Why was the elevated vibration not acted upon?
→ Vibration reading of 5.2 mm/s was flagged as "monitor" not "action required."
  The alarm threshold was set at 7.0 mm/s per OEM manual.
  However, the trend from 2.1 mm/s (baseline) to 5.2 mm/s in 4 months
  indicated accelerated degradation that was not analyzed.

WHY 5: Why was the trend not analyzed?
→ No automated trending system. Vibration data collected on paper forms.
  No comparison with previous readings during analysis.
  Vibration analyst works on contract basis, visits quarterly.

================================================================
4. ROOT CAUSES IDENTIFIED
================================================================

RC-1 (PRIMARY): Use of non-OEM refurbished bearings with shorter service life
RC-2 (CONTRIBUTING): Lack of vibration trend analysis capability
RC-3 (CONTRIBUTING): Delay in acting on elevated vibration readings
RC-4 (SYSTEMIC): No spare parts inventory management for critical equipment

================================================================
6. LESSONS LEARNED
================================================================

1. Vibration TREND is more important than absolute value. A reading of
   5.2 mm/s may be below alarm, but a rise from 2.1 to 5.2 in 4 months
   is a critical degradation signal.

2. Non-OEM spare parts for critical rotating equipment introduce
   unpredictable failure modes and shorter service life.

3. Paper-based condition monitoring data prevents effective trending.
   Digital systems are essential for predictive maintenance.

4. Night shift failures are particularly dangerous as response capability
   is limited. Critical equipment should have automated protection trips.
