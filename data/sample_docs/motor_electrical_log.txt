--- SCOPE ---
This report covers electrical condition monitoring of all motors in the Unit 3 pumping system and associated equipment. Testing was performed as part of the quarterly predictive maintenance program in accordance with IEEE 43-2013 (Insulation Resistance Testing) and IS 325:1996 (Three-Phase Induction Motors).

--- MOTOR CONDITION DATA ---

MOTOR TAG: M-101
Coupled To: P-101 (Centrifugal Feed Pump)
Rating: 75 kW, 415V, 3-Phase, 50 Hz, 2960 RPM
Frame: 280M | Insulation Class: F (155°C)
Manufacturer: Crompton Greaves | Year: 2018

Insulation Resistance (Megger Test @ 500V DC):
  Phase U-E: 185 MΩ (Previous: 320 MΩ — DECLINING TREND)
  Phase V-E: 210 MΩ (Previous: 340 MΩ — DECLINING TREND)
  Phase W-E: 195 MΩ (Previous: 310 MΩ — DECLINING TREND)
  Polarization Index (PI): 2.1 (Minimum acceptable: 2.0 — BORDERLINE)
  Assessment: MARGINAL. IR values have dropped 40% in 6 months. Possible moisture ingress or winding contamination. Recommend thermal imaging and surge comparison test within 30 days.

Winding Temperature (during P-101 operation at 85% load):
  Phase U: 128°C
  Phase V: 132°C
  Phase W: 141°C (WARNING — approaching Class F limit of 155°C)
  Assessment: Phase W winding temperature is 9°C higher than Phase U. This asymmetry suggests a potential inter-turn fault developing in Phase W, or a rotor eccentricity issue. Note: This elevated winding temperature CORRELATES with the high vibration observed on P-101 — mechanical vibration from bearing degradation can cause rotor eccentricity, which increases winding temperature non-uniformly.

Current Draw vs Nameplate:
  Nameplate FLA: 128A
  Measured (at 85% load): 118A (Phase U), 119A (Phase V), 124A (Phase W)
  Assessment: Phase W current imbalance of 4.2% exceeds the 3% threshold per NEMA MG-1. Consistent with elevated winding temperature finding.

Bearing Vibration (Motor Bearings):
  Drive End (DE): 4.8 mm/s (WARNING — above 4.5 mm/s alarm threshold)
  Non-Drive End (NDE): 3.1 mm/s (Normal)
  Assessment: DE bearing vibration is elevated. This may be transmitted vibration from P-101's failing pump bearings through the coupling, or the motor DE bearing itself may be degrading. Coupling alignment should be verified after P-101 bearing replacement.

Power Factor: 0.82 (at 85% load) — Normal for this motor type and loading.

Last Predictive Maintenance: 12-Mar-2024 (Thermography + Vibration)
Next Scheduled: 12-Sep-2024

RECOMMENDATION FOR M-101: HIGH PRIORITY. Motor should be removed for workshop testing (surge comparison, core loss test) when P-101 is taken down for bearing replacement. The declining IR and winding temperature asymmetry could lead to a winding failure if left unaddressed. Budget estimate for rewinding if required: ₹2,50,000.
