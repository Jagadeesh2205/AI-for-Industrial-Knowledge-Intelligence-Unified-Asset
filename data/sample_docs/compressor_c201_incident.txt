INCIDENT REPORT — COMPRESSOR C-201 BEARING FAILURE
===================================================
Report Number: IR-2022-031
Date of Incident: 08/04/2022
Equipment: Reciprocating Compressor C-201 (Dresser-Rand)
Location: Unit-3, Compressor House

================================================================
SUMMARY
================================================================

Compressor C-201 experienced main bearing failure during normal operation.
The failure was traced to the use of non-OEM bearings installed during
the previous maintenance cycle (PM in January 2022).

The failure pattern was similar to Pump P-101 failures:
- Elevated vibration trending over 3 months
- Bearing temperature rise from 55°C to 82°C over 2 weeks
- Final failure: bearing seizure and automatic trip

Key difference: C-201 has an automatic trip system at 85°C which
prevented catastrophic damage. P-101 did not have this feature at the time.

================================================================
ROOT CAUSE
================================================================

Primary: Non-OEM bearing (local manufacturer) used during PM
- Bearing metallurgy was softer than OEM specification
- Surface finish was rougher, leading to accelerated wear
- Service life: 3 months (vs OEM rated 18 months for C-201)

Contributing: Procurement policy allowed lowest-cost vendor for bearings
- No specification verification process for critical spares

================================================================
CORRECTIVE ACTIONS
================================================================

1. All non-OEM bearings in C-201 replaced with Dresser-Rand OEM bearings
2. Policy updated: Critical rotating equipment must use OEM-specified bearings
3. Incoming quality inspection added for all bearing purchases
4. C-201 automatic trip system verified and calibrated
5. RECOMMENDATION: Install automatic bearing temperature trip on P-101
   (Currently P-101 only has alarm, no automatic trip)

================================================================
CROSS-REFERENCE
================================================================

This incident is related to P-101 incident IR-2022-047 (September 2022).
Both incidents share the same root cause: non-OEM bearings.
Lessons from this C-201 incident were not effectively communicated
to prevent the later P-101 failure — identified as a systemic gap
in lesson-learned communication.
