DOCUMENT ID: PROC-RCA-001
TITLE: Standard Operating Procedure: Root Cause Analysis (RCA) Methodology
CATEGORY: operating_procedure
DATE: 2023-01-10

--- 1. PURPOSE ---
This procedure defines the standard methodology for conducting Root Cause Analysis (RCA) on equipment failures, safety incidents, and environmental excursions at the facility.

--- 2. TRIGGER CRITERIA ---
An RCA must be initiated within 48 hours for any event meeting one or more of the following criteria:
- Equipment failure leading to production loss > $50,000.
- Any rotating equipment failure requiring replacement of major components (e.g., bearings, shafts, impellers).
- Any safety incident requiring medical treatment.
- Any environmental release exceeding reportable quantities.
- Repeated failures of the same equipment tag (3 or more times in a 12-month period).

--- 3. APPROVED METHODOLOGIES ---
The facility uses a combined approach for RCA:

3.1 The 5-Whys (Initial Analysis)
Used to quickly drill down from the symptom to the fundamental cause.
Example: 
Symptom: Pump stopped.
Why 1? Motor tripped on overload.
Why 2? Shaft seized.
Why 3? Bearing failed.
Why 4? Lack of lubrication.
Why 5? Lube oil pump failed. (Root Cause)

3.2 Ishikawa (Fishbone) Diagram (Deep Dive)
Used when the 5-Whys is insufficient or when multiple contributing factors are suspected. The analysis must cover the 6 Ms:
- Man (Personnel, training, human error)
- Machine (Equipment, design, maintenance)
- Material (Raw materials, consumables like lube oil)
- Method (Procedures, processes)
- Measurement (Sensors, calibration, monitoring)
- Mother Nature (Environment, ambient conditions)

--- 4. DOCUMENTATION AND TRACKING ---
All RCAs must be documented using the standard RCA Template (Form F-RCA-01). 
Corrective and Preventative Actions (CAPAs) derived from the RCA must be entered into the CMMS (Computerized Maintenance Management System) and tracked to completion.

--- 5. SPECIFIC DIRECTIVES FOR ROTATING EQUIPMENT ---
For rotating equipment (compressors, centrifugal pumps, turbines), the RCA must explicitly investigate:
- Alignment records (laser alignment vs. dial indicator)
- Vibration history and spectral analysis (1x, 2x, BPF frequencies)
- Lubrication analysis (oil viscosity, particulate count, water content)
- OEM manual recommendations for preventative maintenance intervals.
