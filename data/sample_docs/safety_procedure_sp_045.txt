STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
============================
Document: SP-045 (Rev 3)
Title: LOCKOUT-TAGOUT (LOTO) Procedure for Rotating Equipment
Effective Date: 01/04/2023
Next Review: 01/04/2024
Approved By: K. Venkataraman (Plant Manager)

================================================================
1. PURPOSE
================================================================

This procedure establishes the minimum requirements for the lockout/tagout
of energy sources for rotating equipment maintenance in Unit-3.
Compliance with this procedure is mandatory per Factory Act 1948,
Section 21 and OISD-STD-105.

================================================================
2. SCOPE
================================================================

Applies to all rotating equipment including:
- Centrifugal pumps (P-101, P-102, P-103, P-201, P-202)
- Compressors (C-201, C-202)
- Blowers (B-101, B-102)
- Agitators (AG-101, AG-201)

================================================================
3. RESPONSIBILITIES
================================================================

3.1 Authorized Person (Maintenance Technician)
- Applies lockout/tagout devices
- Verifies zero energy state
- Performs maintenance work

3.2 Affected Person (Operator)
- Notifies when equipment is ready for isolation
- Confirms equipment status before restart

3.3 Permit Issuer (Shift Supervisor)
- Issues Permit to Work (PTW)
- Verifies LOTO application
- Authorizes removal of LOTO after work completion

================================================================
4. PROCEDURE — LOCKOUT APPLICATION
================================================================

Step 1: NOTIFICATION
- Notify all affected operators and adjacent work areas
- Obtain Permit to Work (PTW) from Shift Supervisor
- Verify standby equipment is available and operational

Step 2: EQUIPMENT SHUTDOWN
- Bring equipment to normal shutdown condition
- Follow equipment-specific shutdown procedure
- For P-101: Close discharge valve V-101-D, then suction valve V-101-S
- Allow 5 minutes for system to depressurize

Step 3: ISOLATION
- Isolate electrical supply at MCC (Motor Control Center) Panel-7
- Apply lock with personal padlock and danger tag
- Isolate suction and discharge block valves
- Apply chain locks on isolation valves
- Drain pump casing if required (open drain valve DV-101)

Step 4: VERIFICATION
- Attempt to start equipment from local panel (must not start)
- Verify zero pressure on discharge gauge
- Verify zero voltage at motor terminals using multimeter
- Verify mechanical isolation (shaft does not rotate by hand — for pumps only if safe)

Step 5: DOCUMENTATION
- Record all lockout points on PTW form
- Take photograph of LOTO arrangement
- Inform control room of isolation status

================================================================
5. PROCEDURE — LOCKOUT REMOVAL
================================================================

Step 1: WORK COMPLETION VERIFICATION
- Verify all tools and materials removed from equipment
- Verify all guards and safety devices reinstalled
- Verify all personnel clear of equipment

Step 2: RESTORATION
- Remove drain connections and close drain valve DV-101
- Open suction valve V-101-S
- Slowly open discharge valve V-101-D (to prevent water hammer)

Step 3: LOCKOUT REMOVAL
- Only the authorized person who applied the lock may remove it
- Remove personal padlock and danger tag
- Re-energize electrical supply at MCC

Step 4: STARTUP
- Follow equipment-specific startup procedure
- For P-101: Prime pump, start, verify discharge pressure 4.0-4.5 bar
- Monitor vibration and temperature for 30 minutes after startup
- Record startup parameters on PTW form

Step 5: PTW CLOSURE
- Return PTW to Shift Supervisor with all signatures
- Update maintenance log

================================================================
6. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
================================================================

6.1 In case of fire or gas leak during LOTO work:
- STOP all work immediately
- Do NOT remove LOTO devices
- Evacuate to assembly point AP-3
- Notify control room on emergency channel

6.2 If lock owner is unavailable:
- Plant Manager authorization required for lock removal
- Two witnesses must be present
- Equipment must be verified safe before lock removal
- Document in incident log
