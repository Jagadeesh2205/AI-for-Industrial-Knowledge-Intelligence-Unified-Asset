SECTION 2: INSTALLATION REQUIREMENTS
================================================================

2.1 Foundation
- Level concrete pad, minimum 150mm thick
- Anti-vibration mounts recommended for installations above 2nd floor
- Alignment tolerance: 0.05mm (angular), 0.08mm (parallel)

2.2 Piping
- Suction pipe: DN100, minimum straight length 5D before pump
- Discharge pipe: DN80, non-return valve required within 2D
- Maximum suction lift: 7m (NPSH required: 2.8m)

================================================================
SECTION 3: PREVENTIVE MAINTENANCE SCHEDULE
================================================================

3.1 Daily Checks (Operator)
- Visual check for leaks at seals and flanges
- Check bearing temperature (normal: <65°C, alarm: 75°C, trip: 85°C)
- Check vibration (normal: <4.5 mm/s, alarm: 7.0 mm/s, trip: 11.0 mm/s)
- Verify discharge pressure is within 4.0-4.5 bar
- Check motor current (rated: 22A, alarm: >25A)

3.2 Monthly Checks
- Grease bearing housings (Shell Gadus S2 V220 AD 2, 15g per housing)
- Check coupling alignment
- Inspect foundation bolts
- Clean cooling fins on motor

3.3 Quarterly Checks
- Vibration spectrum analysis (required per OISD-116)
- Oil analysis on bearing lubrication
- Thermographic survey of motor and bearings
- Check control panel and protection devices

3.4 Annual Maintenance (12-Month Interval — CRITICAL)
- Replace DE and NDE bearings (SKF 6310-2Z)
  WARNING: Bearing life at rated load is 12 months.
  Operating beyond 12 months significantly increases failure risk.
- Inspect mechanical seal, replace if wear exceeds 2mm
- Inspect impeller for erosion/corrosion
- Full shaft alignment verification
- Hydrostatic test at 1.5x rated pressure
- Update maintenance log with all findings

================================================================
SECTION 4: FAILURE MODES AND TROUBLESHOOTING
================================================================

4.1 High Vibration
Possible Causes:
a) Bearing wear or damage — Check bearing frequencies in spectrum
b) Shaft misalignment — Laser alignment required
c) Impeller imbalance — Remove and dynamically balance
d) Cavitation — Check NPSH available vs required
e) Foundation looseness — Inspect and re-torque anchor bolts

4.2 High Bearing Temperature
Possible Causes:
a) Insufficient lubrication — Re-grease per schedule
b) Bearing wear — Replace bearings (see 3.4)
c) Misalignment — Realign pump-motor
d) Overloading — Check motor current vs rated
e) Ambient temperature effects — Ensure adequate ventilation

WARNING: If bearing temperature exceeds 85°C, STOP THE PUMP IMMEDIATELY.
Continued operation will result in catastrophic bearing failure and
potential shaft seizure.

4.3 Seal Failure
Possible Causes:
a) Dry running (no liquid in pump) — MOST COMMON CAUSE
b) Abrasive particles in fluid
c) Thermal shock
d) Incorrect installation
e) Seal face wear beyond limit

4.4 Loss of Performance (Low Flow/Pressure)
Possible Causes:
a) Impeller wear/erosion
b) Air entrainment in suction
c) Blocked suction strainer
d) Worn wear rings
e) Incorrect impeller rotation direction
